Adidas and Argentina Extend Partnership

Adidas has officially extended its kit deal with the Asociación del Fútbol Argentino (AFA) until 2038, marking a significant milestone in their long-term partnership. This extension will bring the total duration of their collaboration to 38 years.
Read More

The existing contract, which was set to expire in 2026, has now been prolonged by an additional 12 years. AFA President Claudio Tapia expressed his enthusiasm about the renewed agreement, emphasizing the enduring success and growth opportunities it presents for both parties. Tapia stated, "Our partnership spans several decades and this new long-term commitment allows us as an organisation to drive the success of our teams and invest in our communities. We are proud of the recent successes we have enjoyed together, but we will continue to focus on the growth of our game and our performance on the pitch... in this and many other ways, Adidas is the perfect partner for us."

By 2038, the partnership between Adidas and Argentina will have reached a remarkable 38 years, having started in 2001.

In contrast, other major national teams like France, Brazil, the Netherlands, and Belgium will see their contracts with their current kit suppliers expire in 2026, necessitating new agreements starting in 2027.
